    Common problems that characterises street children lives

    Basic Needs and Survival:

    Street children often struggle to meet their basic needs such as food, shelter, clean water, and healthcare. They may scavenge for food from garbage bins, beg for money, or engage in small informal jobs to survive.

    Lack of Education:

    Access to education is limited for street children. Without proper schooling, they miss out on opportunities for personal growth and skill development, perpetuating the cycle of poverty.

    Health Risks:

    Street children are exposed to various health risks due to their living conditions. They lack access to clean water and sanitation facilities, making them vulnerable to diseases. They might also face challenges in accessing medical care and are at a higher risk of drug addiction and substance abuse.

    Violence and Exploitation:

    Street children are often subjected to violence and exploitation. They might face physical, sexual, and emotional abuse from peers, adults, or even law enforcement. Additionally, they can become victims of human trafficking, forced labor, and commercial sexual exploitation.

    Social Isolation:

    Street children often lack a supportive social network. They might experience feelings of loneliness, rejection, and a lack of belonging, which can lead to psychological and emotional distress.

    Legal Issues:

    Many street children engage in activities that are illegal, such as begging or petty theft, to survive. This puts them at risk of being arrested and facing legal consequences, which can further disrupt their lives.

    Limited Opportunities:

    Without education or a stable home environment, street children have limited opportunities to break the cycle of poverty. They often lack the skills and resources needed to find stable employment and become self-sufficient.

    Mental Health Challenges:

    The constant stress, trauma, and uncertainty of street life can lead to mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
